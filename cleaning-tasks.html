  • Cleaning the kitchen
    • Getting started

      It is important to take stock of the situation before starting the cleaning process.

    • Supplies

      Specific supplies are needed for cleaning the kitchen.

    • Baking soda paste

      In some cases, you will need to create a baking soda paste to clean and deodorize the household surfaces.

    • Cleaning tasks

      Complete the following tasks for cleaning the customer's kitchen.

      • Removing the garbage

        Clear the room of opened and expired food products and other debris, so you have no obstacles.

      • Cleaning the cabinets

        Clean the inside and outside of all kitchen cabinets.

      • Cleaning the inside of the range

        Clean the oven, including shelves and other related parts.

      • Cleaning the glass stove top

        Clean the stove top and surrounding area.

      • Cleaning the inside of the refrigerator and microwave oven

        Clean the interior of the refrigerator and microwave oven, which often have sticky spills.

      • Cleaning the outside of stainless steel appliances

        Clean the outside of stainless steel appliances, such as the refrigerator, range, dishwasher, and microwave oven.

      • Cleaning the granite countertops

        Clean the entire surface of the granite countertops paying special attention to the grit that collects in the corners and creases.

      • Cleaning the glass table and chairs

        Clean the glass table and plastic chairs.

      • Cleaning the stainless steel sink basin

        After you have completed most of the tasks that require use of the sink, clean the sink basin.

      • Cleaning the kitchen floor

        After you have completed the majority of the kitchen tasks, clean the floor. By cleaning the floor last, you do not have to worry about inadvertently walking on the freshly-cleaned floor and potentially leaving scuff marks or foot prints.
